    Profession: Actor

    Biography: Josh Hutcherson is an American actor. His career spans from early childhood roles to the international recognition as Peeta Mellark in The Hunger Games film series (2012-2015). Hutcherson has also been involved in independent cinema and production while actively supporting the LGBT community and engaging in political activism.

    Beginning acting at the age of eight, Josh Hutcherson moved to Los Angeles with his mother to pursue his career. His early work consisted of commercials and minor television roles. His breakthrough came with family films such as Little Manhattan (2005), Zathura: A Space Adventure (2005), Bridge to Terabithia (2007), and Journey to the Center of the Earth (2008), winning him Young Artist Awards.

    With his casting as Peeta Mellark in The Hunger Games series, Hutcherson gained international fame, receiving MTV Movie Awards and a People's Choice Award. Concurrently, he worked on Journey 2: The Mysterious Island (2012), voiced a character in Epic (2013), and pursued roles in independent films. As a producer, he contributed to Detention (2011), The Forger (2012), and Escobar: Paradise Lost (2015).

    More recently, Hutcherson starred in Future Man (2017-2020) and voiced the lead in Ultraman (2019â€“2023). He returned to mainstream cinema with the films Five Nights at Freddy's (2023) and The Beekeeper (2024). His advocacy work includes the "Straight But Not Narrow" campaign, and he has publicly supported Bernie Sanders for U.S. president in 2016 and 2020.

    Hutcherson's cultural impact was further solidified by a viral YouTube video in 2014. Recognition for his career and social work includes additional Young Artist Awards, MTV Movie Awards, the GLAAD Vanguard Award, and the Young Humanitarian Award. Influenced by actors like Jake Gyllenhaal and Philip Seymour Hoffman, Hutcherson has also been commissioned as a Colonel of the Commonwealth of Kentucky.

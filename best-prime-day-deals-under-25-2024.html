    When is Prime Day 2024

    Although there are a few early Prime Day deals leaking out already, Amazon’s 10th Prime Day event kicks off at 3am EST on July 16th, and ends at 3am EST on July 17th.

    Are Prime Day deals the same on both days?

    Definitely not! The majority of Prime Day deals will start on July 16th, but others will begin on the 17th.

    In addition, there will be many “Limited Deals” popping up throughout the event. These special deals feature extra-low prices, but are only active for a few hours and have limited supply. If you find a great Limited Deal item, jump on it before it disappears!

    How does Prime Day work?

    Prime Day is known for its impressive discounts across all categories on thousands of items available on Amazon, but only shoppers with an active Amazon Prime subscription have access to these special sales.

    In addition to the big Prime Day deals, Prime members also have access to Prime Video (normally $8.99,) free shipping on thousands of Prime items, free video games via Prime Gaming, and more.

    Brand-new subscribers can test out Prime with a free 30-day membership, and if you’re a fan, Amazon Prime is offered for $14.99 per month, or $139 for a yearly subscription.

    Do you need to be an Amazon Prime member to shop Prime Day deals?

    Yes, because only Prime members have access to Amazon’s Prime Day sales.

    How to prepare for the Prime Day sale

    For starters, sign up for a subscription of Amazon Prime, and input your preferred payment information and address immediately. Savvy shoppers will appreciate the ability to check out from their cart quickly to take advantage of swiftly ending deals and popular items that may sell out quickly.

    Tips for finding the best Prime Day deals

    Prime Day may seem wild for anyone searching for the best deals for their needs for the first time, but there are a few key tips and tricks that will make your shopping experience a bit easier:

    Before you begin, make a list of items you would buy if they were discounted to under $25, such as snacks to restock the pantry, cool gifts for upcoming birthdays and holidays, and fun treats for yourself because you deserve it. Knowing what you want before you dive in will make navigating Prime Day a breeze.

    Plan on revisiting Amazon throughout the 16th and 17th. Deals come and go frequently during Prime Day, and it’s worth coming back a few times so you don’t miss out on something special.

    Buying exclusively items marked with the Prime icon will help to streamline your shopping even further. There are thousands of items marked with the “Prime” logo, which indicates that they are sold either directly from Amazon or a trusted third-party seller, and will ship for free.

    What retailers offer the best Prime Day alternatives?

    Although Amazon should be your go-to shopping destination from July 16th through the 17th, it doesn’t hurt to compare prices on items in your cart before checking out just in case a competitor has an even better offer. Some of the top retailers to keep an eye on for bonus sales include Best Buy, Walmart, Kohl’s, Chewy, Costco, Wayfair, and Lowe’s.

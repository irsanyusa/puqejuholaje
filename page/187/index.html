Across Western Europe, FIFA still suspected of corruption

01 Jan 0001, 00:00

Three quarters of Britons, Germans, Spaniards and French people believe there has probably been corruption in FIFA in recent yearsIn late 2010 FIFA made the controversial decision to award the 2018 World Cup tournament to Russia and the 2022 tournament to Qatar. Allegations of corruption, vote-swapping and government interference followed the results, and concerns were raised about awarding such prestigious tournaments to countries with such poor human rights records. Now, 12 years on, YouGov data shows that FIFA still suffers from widespread public suspicion that there has been wrongdoing in the management of the organisation.
